The Effect of Pseudomonas Fluorescens on Fusarium Oxysporum

Bio Research Project - James Chen

Background

Pseudomonas Fluorescens have been used on a variety of fungi in the past and have displayed inhibitory effects on general fungal growth. However, the effects of Pseudomonas Fluorescens have not been extensively studied on the Fusarium Oxysporum fungi. Similar studies that used Pseudomonas have suggested that Pseudomonas Fluorescens could potentially serve to screen pathogen effectors of another fungus, Puccinia Graminis, in wheat , but not much research has been done on the inhibition of the Fusarium Oxysporum fungi by Pseudomonas Fluorescens.

Rationale

Pseudomonas Fluorescens is a type of bacteria that belongs to the Pseudomonas genus and shows promising biocontrol properties. This type of bacteria produces Phenazine-type antifungal compounds when exposed to certain types of fungus, such as Fusarium Oxysporum: a fungus that causes Fusarium Wilt in a variety of crops. The purpose of this study is to test whether the introduction of Pseudomonas Fluorescens into an already established fungal colony of Fusarium Oxysporum would inhibit and reduce the growth and area of the colony.

Result

The hypothesis was not supported by the data. The two groups had a statistically significant difference in the average and total area of the colonies, with the experimental group having the larger average and total colony area contrary to the hypothesis. This indicated that for already established fungal colonies, the antifungal effect of Pseudomonas Fluorescens had been greatly reduced or eliminated.
